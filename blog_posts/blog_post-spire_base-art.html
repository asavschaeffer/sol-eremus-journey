 Inspect: ankle, leg & footer

  Because of the visual theme of the website being this "jack and the beanstalk" type design, I had some challenges blending the bottom and the top with a trunk of variable length. I created a trunk that could be repeated vertically, but that had the drawback of being unable to meet up cleanly with the topmost and bottommost elements. To resolve that, I used some clouds and water to cover the places where the top and the base would meet the trunk, which makes 5 vertical overlapping parts.

  If you inspect any page, you can see that the 5 parts are named header, neck, body, leg, ankle, and footer. The portion that sits above the footer would be the ankle, which is blended into the body using the leg. The body is blended into the header using the neck, which sits just below the navigation links.

  For me it is super helpful to keep things organized if there is a mapping of the idea onto another concept, almost like an analogy.

Inspiration

  One of my favorite activities as a kid was outdoors survival. I went to camp and learned how to make arrows, start fires, walk quietly around animals, forage berries, etc. I was obsessed with My Side of the Mountain by Jean Craighead George. My friends Connor and Quinn Lydon lived Felton, California at the time and shared my love for going nuts in the forest. We had build a fort inside a burned out tree trunk by covering the roof with a lattice of sticks and ferns. We each had a personal stick carved with tribal markings

  A lot of time has passed since then, but the hollowed tree stayed with me. It's a bit of a digression, but I have this idea that living in a tree trunk is homely because it is totally yonic and the tree entering into the earth with its roots is something like a sexual interchange between the archetypal mother and the phallic tree. A hollow at the base of the tree is a dark space, a cave, an entrance to the underworld. The tree is the linga, and the hollow is the yoni base. One of the foremost goals of Sol Eremus is to reverse the demonization of the feminine caused by the Garden of Eden incident, and so at the base of the tree, inside the hollow, is a whirlpool where a snake can be found. The feminine and natural must be re-instated as an equal to the masculine and civilized, before we dominate and subjugate her beyond the tipping point. My vision of the combination of the feminine and masculine archetypes is the great tree trunk, which is phallic and masculine, resting on a cave of water, which is yonic and feminine.

  I was on a walk in the UBC Botanical Gardens at the very end of my senior year with the talented, beautiful, and wise Caylee Watrin and was struck by two tree trunks and knew they would be the first concept art for the yoni that serves as a base for the linga ascending to the Mount of Sol.

IRL to Digital

  I cut out the background and the different layers of the tree trunks. There are lots of AI available nowadays like Remove.ai, Remove.bg and inPixio, but I have found that these usually charge to get a good resolution result and usually you have to touch up the result anyway. So I use a combination of the fuzzy select, scissors select, foreground select color select tools in Gimp, and then I re-arranged the different pieces.

  I tried my best to put the features of the two trees together into one that resembled a hollow trunk, with a whirlpool at the center, and a spire rising to the skies. There are a few variations because I quickly realized early on that, at this point of the process, the art isn't about what you want it to look like. It's about what the AI sees and transforms it into.

  This work here clearly has a number of problems and I'm excited for you to see how much I have improved just in the timeline of this project. That being said, it ended up being just enough to get over the first plateau: the AI generating a better conceptual mockup than the person's first mockup.

AI art aside

  Let's just talk about AI for a moment. In the last year, we have gone from basically worthless AI art to near perfection. It is true that this explosion of progress is due to the Ai companies' scraping of all the art on the internet, and their profit without revenue share for the original artists is a serious choice that I cannot support. For that reason, I never pay for AI art. On my Discord, I have a thread which I keep updated with the current free AI art software that I am using. There is also an article about it on this website.

  At the moment of writing this article I can recommend:

  • Stablecog
    • generates 1280*720px for 1 credit
    • upscale to 5120*2880px for 1 credit
    • 30 credits each day
    • best for transforming source image to remove signs of photoshop
  • NightCafe
    • generates 512*512px for 0 credits
    • upscale by 11x for 3 credit
    • get a couple credits each day
    • More variety of stable diffusion variations
    • inpainting beta
    • prompt presets
  • Dream
    • generates one image at 960*1568 for free
    • huge amount of artistic styles
    • impressive when generating art from text
    • also can generate art from source
    • small but mighty
    • I actually love using this for concept art
    • finalize and publish useless
    • annoying popups constantly
  • freewayML
    • generates one image at 900*1600 for free
    • useful vary by structure, texture, or fine details function
    • generates from image and from text both decently
    • pretty stingy with the credits
    • annoyingly fails to preserve settings

  The process of going from those trees at the botanical garden and what you see at the bottom of the webpage today took about a month. I went back and forth through many of those AI above and GIMP making alterations and refining the prompt settings. If you're interested in learning the details, hold on to your pants.

Wrong Way!

  The biggest errors I made was asking too much of the AI at once. If I could give one recommendation for this process, it would be to take complex ideas piece by piece and use photoshop in between. I am asking for a novel image, so the AI has no ability to cleanly put together hollow tree trunk, stone texture, in the ocean, whirlpool, treatcherous stairs and caves without breaking down.

  I experimented with a variety of source images, and the successful iteration will be shown in the next portion. For these "problem children" of the "second generation", I used a source image(

) that had kept the whirlpool from Stablecog, which I realize now led to a lot of the issues.

  I changed course because I noticed that the AI couldn't transform the whirlpool that appeared vertical into something horizontal.

  Using a source image(

) that had the overall shape I was hoping for, albeit not the texture or the art style, led naturally to more art that was the shape but not the texture I was looking for.

 Sometimes when generating hundreds of images for free, waiting for credits each day, accidentally messing up the prompt and wasting generations, etc. can be soul-draining. When you feel like that, I would recommend taking a moment to look at your wallet and consider paying for an artist.

Glorious Success!

  I went back to the drawing board after lots of failures trying to get the perfect image, and used a more general source image. (

) I actually gave up on the texture of the tree, the overall shape of the hollow trunk, almost everything that I had put so much work into I threw away.

    Something about this image created a dark atmosphere for the Nightcafe. It also outputted some interesting wooden and icy textures that werent strictly rocky or forested, even though they were nowhere present in the source. My success in finding the perfect image in this case is only explainable by luck.

  Somehow this image captured even the whirlpool element of the prompt, albeit subtly. There is the tree texture, hollowed out, climbing into the background, which seems to have been blended with cloud. The foreground is more rocky, and maybe even there is the hint of stairs there in the center.

  It is both magical and suspicious. I don't like to ascribe things to pure luck, so I believe Nightcafe is doing some sort of model training from the image sources. There is no way that it has all of those textures that I stopped introducing when I used the source image(

) because it clearly got those from somewhere else.

Using Overlay and Opacity

  To understand how to alter the source images to get the results you want, all we need to do is understand how the AI sees the source image. Unfortunately, it is easier said than done. I had some success understanding the tricks of this by viewing how the AI can create foreground, background, and object layer masks, but I think it just takes practice using trial and error developing a sense for what the AI deems as one object and what are separate objects.

  Definitely, making sure the colors are well coordinated and blended goes a long way. For this I use the layer mode setting for new objects that I copy paste in, then blend the edges and erase them with opacity. In this case I am adding in

to the side of the cliff. Considering the size of the image, it was a real challenge scaling the stairs to look detailed, realistic and small at the same time!

  Definitely, making sure the colors are well coordinated and blended goes a long way. For this I use the layer mode setting for new objects that I copy paste in, then blend the edges and erase them with opacity.

  It might look like the layer mode change to overlay is making the image disappear, but I have found that the effects of overlay are absolutely necessary in this case. If the stairs are even in the slightest way visible beyond that, they will be transformed into the wildest of different objects. Plus, overlay is a great tool for this because it technically uses the layer below to create the color for the layer. This means that you can really be creative with the objects that you are blending in.

  It also is tempting to copy paste in textures and shapes, then use a masking tool to just alter that portion until the AI does the blending for you. It sounds good in practice, and maybe it is with the right AI model, but I personally did not have any success with masking tools with any AI art software.

This is where I am currently stuck. I cant improve the image beyond the edits that I made.
